SKI19 — a system for the

Challenge

SAWA is a manufacturing company operating in the textile industry, managing a fleet of over 150 machines. The company was grappling with growing operational complexity—the lack of a unified system meant that data on machine status, production, and inventory existed in silos, often in Excel spreadsheets and in employees’ heads.

Key challenge: implement the system without halting production. The production line had to keep running, and the new software had to start delivering value quickly—without months-long consulting implementations.

Solution

We designed SKI19—a dedicated web application that brings together three areas of the company in one place:

  • CRM and sales—customer order processing, transaction history, and fulfillment statuses visible to the entire department.
  • ERP (production planning)—production orders generated directly from customer orders. The planner sees machine availability and can immediately assign tasks.
  • WMS (warehouse) — real-time updates on fabric, thread, and raw material inventory levels. Alerts for low stock levels.
  • Machine integration — collection of production data from over 150 textile machines: operating time, productivity, downtime. Data in the system, not in the heads of production supervisors.

“Four months was a must for us—the season won’t wait. IMTO Software delivered a working system on time and without any drama.”

How we did it

The project required a fast pace without compromising on quality. We started with a 2-week analysis—mapping production processes, talking to foremen and machine operators, and understanding how the production line really works.

We chose a pilot-first approach: in the first 4 months, we focused on the core of the system (CRM + basic ERP + inventory). Integration with machines and reporting modules were added iteratively once the core was stable and in use.

Data migration from Excel spreadsheets to the system took place over weekends—the company didn’t miss a single production shift.

Results

  • First working system delivered in 4 months — on time and on budget
  • Data from 150+ machines visible in one place, without manual collection by foremen
  • Production planning based on actual data, not estimates
  • Elimination of Excel as the primary company management tool
  • The system is operational and continues to be developed to this day
