Project context

eWaterPay (Digital Trading LTD) is a British organization working to address the clean water crisis in Sub-Saharan Africa. The business model is based on building deep wells and providing water to local communities via prepaid miniature IoT tags—similar to transit cards, but for water supply systems.

The SUSWAT project required the development of an IT system capable of planning and managing the entire water infrastructure—from selecting well locations, through installation and commissioning, to daily operational management in the field.

Solution

We designed and built SUSWAT—a comprehensive platform for water system planning:

  • Infrastructure planning module — mapping well locations, planning the coverage of the water distribution network, estimating costs and capacity for a given area.
  • IoT tag management — registration and activation of prepaid tags for users. Each tag is linked to an account, top-up history, and water consumption.
  • Operations dashboard — real-time status overview of all wells in the field: water level, technical condition, water consumption volume, and failure alerts.
  • B2B integrations — a system integrated with external payment platforms and operator systems, enabling account top-ups via SMS and mobile payments.
  • Reporting — consumption reports for individual wells and regions, data for network expansion planning and water resource management.

“This was one of the most challenging projects—the infrastructure operates in areas without reliable internet access, under extreme conditions. IMTO Software delivered a system that works.”

Technical Challenges

The project presented us with unique challenges stemming from the specific nature of the terrain—unstable internet connections, devices operating in harsh climatic conditions, the need to function offline, and data synchronization upon restoring connectivity.

B2B integrations included connecting to local mobile payment operators (popular in Sub-Saharan Africa as the backbone of the financial system), which required knowledge of the specifics of regional APIs and security standards.

Results

  • A system deployed in Sub-Saharan Africa, operating in real-world field conditions
  • Full digitization of water infrastructure management—from planning to daily operations
  • The prepaid IoT tag model enables cashless and intermediary-free access to water
  • Ability to scale the well network without a proportional increase in operating costs
  • Reporting data supporting decisions on network expansion in new locations
