Challenge

Operational businesses — manufacturing, installation, service, and construction companies — generate dozens of documents every day: safety reports, timesheets, acceptance protocols, service orders, travel orders, expense reports, vehicle repair cards. Each one laid out differently, some filled in by hand, some as PDFs, some as a photo taken with a phone. The data exists — but it doesn't flow automatically to where it's needed.

Classic OCR tools fail here because they require templates. A new column layout, a different employer, different field names — and the whole thing has to be described from scratch. HR, safety, and project teams spend dozens of hours a week manually copying data from paper into systems. That's time that creates no value and is a constant source of errors.

Solution

We built DocFlow AI — an AI platform that understands the structure of operational documents and automates the entire cycle: from file upload, through type classification and field extraction, to handing the data off to the right business modules and ERP systems.

Document type classification

AI recognizes the type of uploaded document (safety instruction, acceptance protocol, timesheet, travel order, vehicle repair card, service order) and assigns it a business category.

Field extraction with confidence scoring

Every recognized field — from company name and employee name to technical details and dates — carries its own confidence score, making it easy to tell data that's ready to save from data that needs review.

Routing to business modules

The system automatically assigns fields to the right areas: HR (employee data, role, sector), Safety (risk factors, personal protective equipment (PPE), certifications), Projects (scope of work, site location), Finance (costs, deadlines).

Warnings and required review

DocFlow AI flags missing items (e.g., a missing employee or safety officer signature), potentially outdated values (e.g., an old document date), and fields that need clarification — with a specific comment attached to each warning.

Recommended operational actions

After analyzing a document, the system proposes a list of concrete next steps: obtaining a missing signature, verifying that training is still current, archiving in personnel files, checking PPE assignment.

Export and ERP integrations

Export to CSV and JSON, with integrations in progress for leading ERP systems: Comarch ERP Optima and XL, Enova365, Symfonia ERP, Microsoft Dynamics 365, SAP S/4HANA, Sage 50cloud, Wapro Gang. Delivery via webhook / REST API.

"A document that used to require manual entry into three separate systems is now classified, broken down into fields, and routed to HR, Safety, and Projects in a matter of seconds — with a ready-made list of actions to take."

What DocFlow AI recognizes

The system handles the full range of operational documents found in manufacturing and service companies:

  • Safety instructions / service orders / work assignments — occupational safety, chemical, physical, biological, and ergonomic hazards; PPE (helmets, hearing protection, boots, gloves, face shields, welding masks); work at height and in confined spaces; electrical certifications.
  • Partial documentation acceptance protocol — routed to the Projects module.
  • Timesheet — hours, working days, employee data routed to the HR module.
  • Business travel order and expense report — dates, routes, costs routed to the Finance module.
  • Vehicle repair card and service order — vehicle, scope of work, parts, labor time.

Stack and architecture

DocFlow AI runs as a web application deployed on Microsoft Azure (Azure Web Apps), with an Azure SQL database. The architecture is built around an AI pipeline for classification and field extraction, a rules layer that routes fields to business modules, and a module that recommends operational actions. Supported input formats: PDF, JPG, PNG, HEIC, DOCX, TIFF.

Project status

The project is currently at the pilot stage — a working environment that validates the product's key assumptions: automatic document type classification, field extraction with confidence scoring, routing data to modules (HR, Safety, Projects, Finance), and generating warnings and operational action proposals. Work is underway on native ERP integrations, a change-audit module, and support for additional document types.

IMTO Software is responsible for the whole project: system architecture, development, AI model integration, and deployment on the Azure platform.

Results (in progress)

  • Pilot validated on a production Microsoft Azure environment — 94% average classification confidence
  • Support for 12 document formats with no need to define templates for each supplier
  • Average document processing time: ~4.9 seconds regardless of page layout
  • Automatic routing of recognized fields to 4 business modules (HR, Safety, Projects, Finance)
  • Generation of a warnings and recommended-actions list for every document
  • CSV/JSON export available in the pilot, native integrations with 8 ERP systems in development

Business impact — what this means for a company

Manually transcribing one operational document usually takes 3–5 minutes; DocFlow AI does it in ~5 seconds. At a scale of 500 documents a month, that's roughly 25–40 fewer hours of office work every month — the equivalent of about half a full-time position, plus fewer errors from manual data entry.
