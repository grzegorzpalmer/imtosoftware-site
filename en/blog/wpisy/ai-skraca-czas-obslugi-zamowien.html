How does AI reduce order
processing time by 60%?

February 1, 2026 AI Sales Automation

60% isn’t just a number plucked out of thin air. It’s a result achieved by one of our clients—a manufacturing company processing dozens of orders daily from B2B customers. A process that used to take a sales representative 12 to 20 minutes per order now takes just 5–7 minutes. Where do these savings come from, and what exactly does AI do?

What does a typical B2B order process look like without AI?

The customer sends an order—via email, a form, or by phone. The sales representative:

  1. Opens the email and reads the order
  2. Checks product availability in the system or Excel
  3. Verifies the price list for this customer (discounts, contracts)
  4. Checks the credit history and credit limit
  5. Manually creates an order confirmation in the system
  6. Sends an email with the confirmation and delivery date
  7. Enters the order into the production plan or forwards it to the warehouse

Each of these steps requires switching between applications. Every switch takes time and increases the risk of error. With 30 orders a day, that’s several hours spent just re-entering data.

What does AI change?

The AI agent doesn’t replace the sales rep. It prepares all the work for them—so that the only decision left is to click “approve.”

Specific workflow after implementation:

  • The agent reads the email—understands the content, extracts the product, quantity, deadline, and customer data. It can even handle non-standard message formats and industry-specific abbreviations.
  • It checks availability—querying the ERP or warehouse system in real time. It sees whether the product is in stock or needs to be manufactured.
  • Retrieves the price list and terms — checks the price for a specific customer, the current discount, and the minimum order quantity.
  • Verifies credit limits — checks the balance and payment history. If the customer has outstanding debt or exceeds the limit — flags the order for manual verification.
  • Prepares a confirmation—generates a ready-to-send email draft with the confirmation, deadline, and details. The sales representative reviews it, makes any necessary corrections, and clicks “send.”
  • Creates an entry in the system — the order is automatically entered into the system without manual re-entry.

Where is that 60% savings?

Time drops from 15 minutes to 6 minutes per order. With 30 orders a day, you save 4.5 hours of work daily. That’s almost a full-time job—which you can dedicate to acquiring new customers instead of re-entering data from existing ones.

On top of that, errors are eliminated: incorrect prices in confirmations, forgotten discounts, and incorrect quantities. Each of these errors leads to a complaint, wasted time, and damage to the customer relationship.

Is it difficult to implement?

It depends on the state of your data and systems. If you have an ERP or an order management system with an API, implementing an order processing agent takes 4–8 weeks. If you mainly use Excel and email, it’s worth organizing your data first—which extends the project timeline but still delivers a return within a quarter.

The most common challenge: customer emails come in various formats, with abbreviations and typos in product names. A good AI agent can handle this—but it requires a training period using real data from your company. That’s why the first 2–4 weeks are a testing phase before the agent operates autonomously.

This isn’t for every company

If you have 3 orders a week from regular customers—investing in AI for order processing doesn’t make sense. If you have a dozen or more orders a day, many B2B customers, and a complex pricing structure—the conversation is worth every minute.
