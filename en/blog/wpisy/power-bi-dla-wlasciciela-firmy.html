Power BI for Business Owners —
Start Seeing Data, Not Just Tables

March 15, 2026 Power BI Analytics Management

Most SME business owners make decisions based on data they receive late, in a format that’s hard to interpret quickly, or not at all—because “someone hasn’t finished the report yet.” Power BI changes that. And it’s simpler than you think.

What is Power BI and why is it worth it?

Power BI is a Microsoft tool for data visualization and analysis. It connects to your data sources—ERP systems, Excel, SQL databases, CSV files—and transforms raw numbers into interactive charts and dashboards. You can view them on a computer, tablet, or phone.

License? Power BI Desktop is free. Power BI Pro (for sharing reports within your company) costs about 45 PLN per user per month—less than lunch.

How does this work in practice?

Imagine that you open your phone in the morning and within 30 seconds you see: yesterday’s revenue vs. the plan, the current inventory status of key raw materials, which production orders are at risk of missing their deadlines, and which machines were operating below standard. Without calling anyone, without waiting for a report.

This isn’t something that’s only possible “after a major BI implementation.” The first useful dashboards can be built within a few weeks if the data is available.

Where does Power BI get its data?

From practically anywhere:

  • Excel and CSV files — even if you don’t have a system, Power BI will pull data directly from spreadsheets
  • ERP and CRM systems — Comarch, Optima, Sage, SAP, Salesforce, and dozens of others have ready-made connectors
  • SQL databases — if you have your own database, the connection is direct
  • Cloud services — Google Analytics, Facebook Ads, e-commerce systems

Data can refresh automatically—every hour, every day, in real time. Once configured, they run on their own.

Where does Power BI deliver the most value in SMEs?

  • Sales — revenue by day, week, or month; breakdown by product, customer, or region; comparison with the plan and the previous year
  • Production — production plan fulfillment, downtime, machine efficiency, raw material consumption vs. standards
  • Finance — cash flow, past-due receivables, margins by product or customer
  • Logistics and inventory — inventory turnover, inventory value, inventory anomalies

What do you need to get started?

Three things: data in some digital format, a Power BI license (the free version is up and running in 5 minutes), and a few hours of work from someone who knows how to put it all together. That person doesn’t have to be a permanent employee at your company—often, a one-time dashboard implementation by an external specialist is sufficient.

The cost of preparing a set of 3–5 dashboards for a typical manufacturing company: PLN 5,000–15,000. Then monthly maintenance—close to zero if the data is stable.

What about AI in Power BI?

Microsoft is actively integrating AI into Power BI. You can already type into the system in Polish today: “show me sales in October broken down by region”—and the system will respond with a report. AI features for anomaly detection, trend forecasting, and smart alerts are available in the standard Pro license.

This makes Power BI not just a reporting tool—but an active decision-making assistant for business owners.
